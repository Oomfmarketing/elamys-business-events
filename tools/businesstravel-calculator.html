Duty of Care — the law requires responsibility for employee safety

Occupational safety law, GDPR and international standards (ISO 31030) require that the employer knows where employees travel and can help in a crisis. Business Travel Barometer 2026: 57 % of CEOs rank Duty of Care as their top priority.

What does Duty of Care mean in practice?

When an employee travels for work, the employer must by law ensure that:

  • The itinerary, accommodation and contact details are known to the employer
  • In a crisis (terrorism, natural disaster, medical) the employee is reachable
  • When travelling to risk areas, the employee receives the necessary briefing and protection
  • A replacement trip or return is arranged quickly if conditions change

Without a centralised travel management company this data is scattered across emails, personal credit cards and employees' memory. Menestys provides one real-time view of every trip.

CSRD reporting — your ESG team saves weeks

The EU CSRD directive (2024 → all companies over 250 employees) requires Scope 3.6 business travel emissions reporting. Manual collection in Excel is weeks of work — Menestys delivers it via API ready-made.

What does Scope 3.6 include?

Business travel greenhouse gas emissions: flights, trains, hotels, rental cars. Each booking needs date, route, distance and CO₂e emissions.
